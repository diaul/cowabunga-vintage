# Cross-Model Vintage Playability Comparison

## Overview

This analysis compares how three different LLMs rated the Vintage playability of every card across the TMNT Magic: The Gathering sets. Each model was given the same prompt and the same CSV input (`TMNT_cards.csv`), and asked to assign a rating from "Very Low" to "Very High" along with a short reasoning.

The goal is to identify cards that at least one model considers potentially relevant for competitive Vintage, and to highlight where models agree or disagree.

## Methodology

- **Input:** 519 cards (including tokens) from sets TMT, TMC, FTMC, and PZA.
- **Models:** ChatGPT 5.2 Thinking (OpenAI), Claude Opus 4.6 (Anthropic), Gemini 2.5 Pro Thinking (Google).
- **Filter:** Only cards rated **Medium**, **High**, or **Very High** by at least one model are included below.
- **Sorting:** Rows are sorted by the highest rating any model gave, then alphabetically by card name.

## Rating Scale

| Rating | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Very High | Likely a staple or frequently played card in competitive Vintage |
| High | Clearly playable in at least one established Vintage archetype |
| Medium | Fringe playable; narrow meta call or niche synergy |
| Low | Very unlikely to see competitive play |
| Very Low | Effectively unplayable in competitive Vintage |

## Results Summary

- **129 cards** were rated Medium or above by at least one model out of 519 total.
- ChatGPT 5.2 was the most generous (7 Very High, 70 High, 92 Medium).
- Gemini 2.5 Pro was selective but confident on known staples (13 Very High, 2 High, 1 Medium).
- Claude Opus 4.6 was the most conservative (0 Very High, 0 High, 7 Medium).
